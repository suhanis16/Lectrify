Problem Statement

Despite the increasing availability of educational resources, the conventional methods of teaching and learning are often criticized for being unengaging and ineffective in helping students retain and apply knowledge. Studies show that students retain only 10-20% of the information they learn through lectures, whereas active learning and engagement can improve retention rates to up to 80% (Freeman et al., 2014). Additionally, traditional testing methods can be time-consuming and may not accurately assess a student's understanding of the material.

About Us

Our team is working on creating a platform, centered around the powerful Protege Effect. Our website will offer users the opportunity to select a topic, level, and board of their choice, such as Mitochondria, 8th Grade, CBSE. With this information, the user will step into the role of a teacher, imparting knowledge and insights to an AI student. But it doesn't stop there. The AI student will be programmed to ask the user questions based on identified gaps in their knowledge. This interactive process will allow the user to engage with the material on a deeper level, reinforcing their understanding of the topic. To further enrich the learning experience, the AI student will take a test with an AI professor, providing valuable feedback to the user in the form of a report card. After this, the user can revisit the topic and reinforce their knowledge, honing their learning and teaching.

Competitor Analysis

Betty's Brain is a computer-based learning environment that utilizes the learning-by-teaching paradigm to engage students in learning about science topics. In Betty's Brain, students teach a computer agent named Betty by constructing a causal model of the systems or processes that make up that science topic (e.g., ecosystems, climate change, or thermoregulation).

SimStudent is an interactive learner, who applies inductive logic programming to learn cognitive skills from examples. SimStudent is customizable (or "programmable") so that we can test various hypotheses to identify factors that affect learning and test when and how such factors affect learning.

Docendo discimus ( “While we teach, we learn” ) - Seneca the Younger

  • Students who serve as tutors for their peers exhibit an increased effort to understand the material, better recall, and improved application. This phenomenon is referred to as “the protégé effect” and has been demonstrated through numerous studies, including those published in 2007 in Science and Intelligence (Kristensen & Bjerkedal, 2007), which found that first-born children typically have higher IQs and attribute this advantage to the time they spend teaching their younger siblings.
  • The motivation to help the student master the material compels student tutors to study the subject more thoroughly. The process of organizing their knowledge in preparation for teaching improves their own understanding and recall, and explaining the information to the agent highlights any gaps in their own thinking. Feedback from the teachable agent further enhances their learning as it encourages them to think and explain the material in different ways and observe the application of their knowledge (Chase et al., 2009).
  • In a 2013 study (Fiorella & Mayer, 2013), a 2014 study(Nestojko et al., 2014), as well as in an experiment conducted in 2018 (Koh et al., 2018) researchers found that participants who were preparing to teach a passage recall more information than those who were expecting to simply take a test. We examined several other studies to understand the Protege Effect and the ways in which teaching can enhance learning to perform a successful implementation of our website.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    One approach is to use a "divide and conquer" strategy by breaking down the task into smaller, more manageable parts. This involves creating a separate table for each textbook and listing all relevant information such as chapters. However, this approach requires a clear understanding of how the data will be organized and managed.

    Another approach is to use an existing database or database platform to store and manage the information. This includes cloud-based database services like Google Cloud SQL or Amazon Web Services' Relational Database Service (RDS) and database management tools like phpMyAdmin or MySQL Workbench. This approach is efficient and ensures the data is well-organized and easily accessible.

    An alternative is to use ChatGPT's database as a resource, as it can provide information on different textbooks and their chapters. However, this may not be the most effective long-term solution.

    To approach GPT's limitations, one option is to introduce our own constraints or parameters to guide the model's responses. For example, if we wanted to use GPT to generate text for a specific board such as CBSE, we could provide the model with a dataset of text specific to that board. This would help to ensure that the responses generated are relevant and appropriate for the given context.

    Additionally, we could fine-tune the GPT model on a smaller dataset of text specific to the board, which could help to improve its performance in generating relevant and accurate responses.

    It's also important to carefully evaluate the responses generated by GPT and to ensure that they are appropriate and accurate before using them in any practical applications.
